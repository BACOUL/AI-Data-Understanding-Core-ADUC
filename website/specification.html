Core Specification

The complete ADUC Core describes the contract itself, the resource, its structure, semantics, identity, context, provenance, uncertainty, relations and usage policy.

Read the source draftOpen the example
Status: This page summarizes a working draft. It is not yet a recognized standard and the complete Core object model and schema family are not yet implemented.

Mission and promise

ADUC defines an open, model-independent contract that allows a data resource to describe its structure, meaning, context, provenance, uncertainty, relations and conditions of use to AI systems, agents and applications.

Two incompatible sources described with ADUC can be understood and compared consistently by multiple AI systems without rebuilding a different semantic integration for every model.

What ADUC is not

  • not a replacement for JSON, CSV, RDF, databases or data lakes;
  • not a universal ontology containing all human knowledge;
  • not an AI model or anticipation engine;
  • not an agent communication protocol;
  • not a replacement for MCP, Croissant, JSON-LD, PROV-O, DQV, ODRL, OpenAPI or CloudEvents;
  • not proof that a declared source or mapping is factually correct;
  • not legal advice, access-control enforcement or a declaration that law permits a requested use.

The ten Core blocks

BlockPurpose

structureRecords, fields, primitive types, keys and structural references.
semanticsField concepts, units, mapping relations, authority, confidence and evidence.
identityEntities, subject fields, identifier schemes and identity uncertainty.
contextTime, timezone, spatial coverage, jurisdiction and operational scope.
provenanceProducer, method, instrument, generation history and derivation.
uncertaintyMeasurement uncertainty, mapping confidence and declared limitations.
relationsBound links between resources, concepts, entities, versions and derivations.
policyODRL-aligned permissions, prohibitions, duties and safe evaluation outcomes.

Epistemic states

ADUC preserves how strongly information is supported. A consumer may not treat an inferred claim as equivalent to a canonical publication.

StatusMeaningRequired behavior
unknownNo supported interpretation exists.Do not invent a target silently.
inferredProduced automatically or heuristically.Preserve confidence, method and evidence.
reviewedExamined by a human reviewer.Preserve reviewer and review evidence.
verifiedValidated under a declared competent process.Preserve verifier and process; do not equate with ownership.
canonicalPublished by the declared source owner or authority.Prefer only when the authority is trusted and no conflict exists.
contestedMultiple supported interpretations exist.Block automatic authoritative selection.
deprecatedReplaced or no longer recommended.Follow a compatible replacement link.

ADR-0005 defines the complete lifecycle. Each accepted domain profile applies the relevant authority, conflict, evidence and lifecycle rules without silently promoting information.

Policy safety boundary

ADR-0013 reuses ODRL and distinguishes executable permissions, prohibitions and duties from classifications, recommendations and legal notices. A public label is not universal permission, free-text purpose matching is blocked, duties require evidence and incomplete or contested policies require human review.

The offline policy evaluator returns only permit, deny, notApplicable, indeterminate or requiresHumanReview. These are profile-evaluation results, not legal permission or enforcement decisions.

Consumer obligations

  1. Validate the declared supported profile.
  2. Preserve semantic target, relation and epistemic status.
  3. Do not hide contested or unresolved information.
  4. Do not convert units without known units and conversion rules.
  5. Do not align times without declared time semantics and timezone.
  6. Do not merge entity identities without sufficient evidence.
  7. Do not infer relation inverse, transitivity or causality.
  8. Do not invent permission, purpose equivalence, duty satisfaction, consent or legal compliance.
  9. Report dimensions that were not evaluated.
  10. Expose conformance errors rather than silently repairing a contract.

Strict MVP

Version 0.1 initially targets JSON and CSV, tabular or record-oriented sources, primitive types, dates, units, identifiers, concepts, elementary provenance, uncertainty, human validation, policy conditions and comparison of two described sources.

Documents, images, live streams, agent memory, autonomous actions, public registry federation and the anticipation engine are outside the strict MVP.

Version 0.1 completion gate

The release is complete only when all twelve deliverables exist: Core specification, full JSON Schema, ten valid examples, ten invalid examples, CLI validator, JSON/CSV compiler, review interface, Core vocabulary, two-source comparison, two-model demonstration, conformance suite and “Try in 5 minutes” documentation.

Relationship to existing standards

ADUC composes established capabilities instead of recreating them. Structural descriptions may reuse JSON Schema or Croissant; semantic identifiers and graphs may use JSON-LD/RDF; provenance may use PROV-O; quality may use DQV; policies use ODRL; relations use SKOS, OWL, PROV-O and domain vocabularies.

Current implementation

Available today: semantic mapping, source binding, units, time, identity, provenance, uncertainty, relation and policy reference profiles with deterministic local evaluators, fixtures and tests; offline JSON-LD/RDF processing; and a provider-neutral conformance harness foundation.
Not yet implemented: frozen normative Core object model, complete Core schema family, unified full-Core validator/comparator, compiler, review UI, value benchmark and two qualifying external AI runs.
