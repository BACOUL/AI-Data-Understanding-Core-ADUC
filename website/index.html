Give every AI system the same understanding of your data.

ADUC is a machine-readable contract that makes a data resource's structure, meaning, context, identity, provenance, uncertainty, relations and use conditions explicit, validatable and portable across AI systems.

Today, meaning is scattered across prompts, code, mappings, connectors and human documentation. ADUC turns that hidden interpretation into an explicit contract that travels with the resource.

The missing layer

Machine-readable is not the same as machine-understood.

01

Syntax exposes values

JSON, CSV and APIs can show that flow is a number. They do not establish what it means, which unit applies or who is authoritative.

02

Interpretation hides in glue

Prompts, ETL jobs, private mappings and connector code rebuild the same understanding for every consumer.

03

Unknowns disappear

Identity gaps, uncertainty, conflicts and use restrictions are often flattened into confident but unsafe assumptions.

AD

ADUC makes the interpretation inspectable

One versioned contract carries the meaning, evidence, limits and conditions to every consumer.

Four explicit steps

From a resource to portable understanding.

ADUC accompanies the source. It does not transform or replace the original bytes.

  1. 01Bind the source

    Identify the exact resource, format, version and digest.

  2. 02Declare understanding

    Describe structure, semantics, identity, context, evidence and limits.

  3. 03Validate and compare

    Run deterministic schema, architecture and profile checks.

  4. 04Deliver the same contract

    Multiple consumers receive the same explicit facts and unknowns.

The complete Core

Ten blocks. One contract. Clear responsibility boundaries.

The minimum interoperable envelope is aduc + resource + structure. Every additional block adds explicit understanding without changing the source.

Real Core syntax

A contract is explicit enough to inspect and strict enough to test.

This excerpt follows the official complete Core example. It uses conceptIri, unitIri, qualified status, authority and evidence references.

Open the complete example →
{{
  "aduc": {{
    "contractId": "urn:aduc:contract:river-r42:2026-07-14",
    "coreVersion": "0.1.0-alpha.0",
    "status": "reviewed"
  }},
  "resource": {{
    "id": "urn:example:resource:river-r42-observations",
    "mediaType": "application/json",
    "digest": "aaaaaaaa…"
  }},
  "structure": {{
    "representation": "record",
    "records": [{{"fields": [{{"name": "flow", "primitiveType": "number"}}]}}]
  }},
  "semantics": {{
    "assertions": [{{
      "subjectRef": "urn:example:field:flow",
      "conceptIri": "https://example.org/vocab/env/WaterDischarge",
      "unitIri": "https://qudt.org/vocab/unit/M3-PER-SEC",
      "status": "reviewed",
      "authority": "reviewed"
    }}]
  }}
}}

Status without marketing blur

Implemented, active, planned and unproven are different states.

Available now

10-block Core, 14 schemas, 11 valid and 15 invalid fixtures, unified validator, deterministic comparator, 24 scenarios, nine evaluators and semantic-profile migration.

In progress

Deterministic formatting of complete validated Core contracts without inference or silent repair.

Planned, not available

JSON/CSV compiler, graphical review, stabilized packages, registry, MCP adapter and official extensions.

Not proven

External interoperability with two independent AI consumers and any absolute “first in the world” claim.

Design principles

Useful to AI. Safe for people to inspect.

AI-first

Machine-readable contracts and deterministic reports, without dependence on one provider.

Human-review-first

Conflicts, unknowns and authority gaps remain visible instead of being silently repaired.

Privacy and security by design

Local schemas, bounded processing and no unapproved remote resolution.

Unknown-safe

Absence of evidence is not converted into equivalence, permission or certainty.
