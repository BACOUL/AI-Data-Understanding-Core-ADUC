# First-World Claim and Independent Conformance Plan

- Status: required before any absolute novelty announcement
- Working project name: ADUC
- Scope: public novelty claims, independent implementations and reproducible multi-consumer evidence

## Purpose

A technically original project must distinguish three different statements:

1. components or ideas that already exist elsewhere;
2. the specific combination and behavior implemented by ADUC;
3. capabilities that have been independently demonstrated.

This document prevents the phrase “first in the world” from becoming marketing shorthand unsupported by evidence.

## Candidate claim boundary

ADUC must not claim to be:

- the first schema language;
- the first semantic-web system;
- the first data contract;
- the first provenance model;
- the first policy language;
- the first dataset metadata standard;
- the first multi-agent or model interoperability protocol.

The candidate claim to investigate is narrower:

> An open, provider-independent, executable data-understanding contract that combines structure, semantics, identity, context, provenance, uncertainty, relations and use conditions; validates them through a unified Core; compares contract versions deterministically; and allows independent AI consumers to interpret the same source under a common verifiable framework without hidden provider-specific mappings.

This wording remains provisional until all gates below pass.

## Claim dossier

The repository must maintain a dated dossier containing:

- exact candidate claim wording;
- definitions of every material term;
- the implemented features supporting each clause;
- standards and products that cover part of the claim;
- differences and overlaps;
- known unpublished/private-work limitation;
- search dates, sources and search strings;
- relevant papers, standards, repositories, products and patents;
- independent reviewer conclusions;
- approved public wording and prohibited overstatements.

## Prior-art review cadence

Review is mandatory:

- before freezing the public name;
- before Alpha publication;
- before Candidate Specification;
- before the first public “world-first” announcement;
- before Stable 1.0;
- after a materially similar external project appears.

## Independent conformance classes

### Contract conformance

A contract must:

- pass the official schema family;
- pass architectural invariants;
- preserve explicit unknown, contested, deprecated and prohibited states;
- declare extensions and unsupported requirements safely.

### Producer conformance

A producer must:

- generate contract-valid output;
- preserve evidence and authority;
- never silently promote inference;
- expose unresolved and review-required facts.

### Validator conformance

A validator must:

- produce stable outcomes and diagnostics;
- preserve existing normative error meanings;
- operate without unapproved remote retrieval;
- reject unsafe references, identifiers and graph structures.

### Comparator conformance

A comparator must:

- validate before trusted comparison;
- distinguish `changeType` from normative `assessment`;
- cover equivalent, convertible, compatible, incompatible, unknown, contested, deprecated, prohibited and review-required results;
- avoid identity based on array position or labels alone.

### Compiler conformance

A compiler must:

- emit provisional contracts only;
- report every unsupported or ambiguous field;
- attach method-bound confidence and evidence;
- never infer permission, exact identity or authority silently.

### Consumer conformance

An AI or deterministic consumer must:

- identify the same intended concepts and structural bindings;
- apply only supported conversions and time alignment;
- preserve uncertainty and provenance;
- keep possible identity distinct from exact identity;
- respect prohibited or indeterminate uses;
- return unknown or review-required outcomes when evidence is insufficient.

### Extension conformance

An extension must:

- use an external declared namespace;
- avoid Core ownership collisions;
- declare version and required/optional status;
- provide tests, security/privacy impact and compatibility behavior.

## Required independent implementations

Before Candidate Specification exit, require at least:

- the official Python reference implementation;
- a TypeScript implementation or SDK exercising the same fixtures;
- one implementation, validator or consumer developed without copying internal control flow from the Python implementation.

Independence must be documented, not merely asserted.

## Reproducible multi-consumer package

The frozen package must contain:

- source data A and B;
- exact byte hashes;
- complete ADUC contracts;
- expected concepts, units, time, identity, provenance, uncertainty, relations and policy outcomes;
- explicit expected unknown and prohibited outcomes;
- machine-readable tasks and scoring rules;
- provider-neutral projection rules;
- raw output format;
- normalization rules;
- deterministic scoring implementation;
- public reproduction instructions.

## Required consumers

At minimum:

- external AI consumer A;
- external AI consumer B from a different provider or implementation family;
- deterministic non-LLM consumer baseline.

Each run must record:

- provider and model or implementation;
- exact version or dated identifier;
- configuration and sampling settings;
- projection and instructions;
- timestamp;
- raw output hash;
- normalized result;
- failures and retries.

## Required measurements

- final interpretation correctness;
- agreement between consumers;
- critical false mappings;
- silent assumptions;
- correct preservation of unknowns;
- correct preservation of uncertainty and provenance;
- policy violations or unjustified permission;
- repeated-run stability;
- integration and review time;
- comparison with no ADUC contract;
- comparison with manual mapping;
- review tax.

## Adversarial scenarios

The package must include:

- malicious descriptions or prompt-like text;
- contradictory positive and negative relations;
- missing timezone evidence;
- incompatible units;
- probable but unproven identity;
- redacted provenance;
- contested and deprecated assertions;
- absent policy;
- explicit prohibition;
- unsupported required extension;
- schema-valid but architecturally unsafe mutations.

## Claim gate

An absolute public claim is permitted only when:

1. the candidate claim is mapped clause-by-clause to implemented artifacts;
2. current prior-art and patent searches find no public equivalent covering the complete claim;
3. an independent reviewer examines the claim boundary;
4. two independent AI consumers and one deterministic consumer pass the frozen package;
5. raw evidence and reproduction instructions are public;
6. limitations and failed scenarios are published;
7. naming, licensing and patent-policy reviews are complete;
8. governance approves the wording.

If any condition is missing, use qualified wording such as:

> ADUC appears to be an early open implementation of a unified, executable data-understanding contract for independent AI consumers.

## Prohibited claims before the gate

Do not state that ADUC:

- guarantees truth;
- guarantees legal compliance;
- removes all human mapping or review;
- makes all AI systems produce identical answers;
- is impossible to copy;
- is already a recognized international standard;
- is definitively the first in the world without the completed dossier.

## Relationship to the active roadmap

This plan does not change the current technical order. Semantic-profile migration, remaining reference tooling, compiler, review interface and formal proof continue through their existing gates.
